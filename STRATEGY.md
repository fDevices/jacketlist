# JacketList — Market Strategy

## What JacketList Is

JacketList is a book discovery site built around two high-intent reader needs:

- **What to read now** — cross-source weekly bestseller rankings
- **What order to read a series in** — clear, trustworthy reading-order guides

The site is positioned as a **practical decision tool**, not a generic book blog. Every page should deliver original, useful content — not templated data pulled from a single source.

---

## Target Audience

| Segment | Description | Commercial Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Intent-driven buyers | Searching for current bestselling or trending books | High |
| Series navigators | Searching "books in order" / "reading order" queries | High |
| Genre readers | Fantasy, thriller, romance, YA, sci-fi — repeat visitors | Medium |
| Casual browsers | Looking for curated discovery | Low |

The first two groups are the commercial priority — they are closest to purchase and most likely to click affiliate links.

---

## Core Growth Thesis

- **Primary traffic engine:** Organic search (SEO-first)
- **Secondary channel:** Social distribution
- **Strongest long-term SEO asset:** The series reading-order library — evergreen, query-specific, commercially aligned
- **Freshness layer:** Weekly bestseller rankings — drives repeat visits and brand differentiation
- **Key requirement:** Every page must offer original explanatory value, not just aggregated book data

---

## Content Strategy

Three content layers:

### 1. Evergreen Utility Pages
- Individual series pages targeting `[series] reading order` and `[series] books in order`
- Each page should include short editorial context explaining *why* the recommended order differs from publication or chronological order where relevant
- Goal: turn a list into a useful reading guide

### 2. Fresh Weekly Pages
- Bestseller rankings using the site's cross-source scoring logic
- Drives repeat visits and establishes recency

### 3. Supporting Editorial Hubs
- Genre-based entry pages
- "Best starting point" guides
- Comparison articles (e.g. "Which Sanderson series to start with")
- Purpose: deepens topical authority, improves internal linking

---

## Monetization Strategy

**Primary:** Amazon Associates affiliate revenue
**Secondary:** Light footer-only ad zone
**Future:** Direct sponsorships (once traffic warrants)

### Rules
- Amazon Associate links must use compliant special links with appropriate identification
- FTC requires clear disclosure of material relationships on all commercial pages
- Affiliate links should feel natural — they appear because the content is close to buying intent, not forced

---

## Advertising Approach

Intentionally conservative. The goal is to protect trust and usability.

**Allowed:**
- Footer ads only

**Never:**
- Pop-ups
- Interstitials
- Sticky interruptions
- Above-the-fold ad clutter
- Pages where ads outweigh content

---

## Trust Assets (Launch Requirements)

Three pages must exist at launch:

1. **Methodology page** — explains how bestseller rankings are compiled (sources, weighting, update frequency)
2. **Editorial Policy** — explains how series reading orders are determined
3. **Amazon Associate disclosure** — visible in the footer and on all commercial pages

These protect credibility with both users and search engines, and make the commercial model feel transparent.

---

## Success Metrics (Year 1)

Primary goal: become the most useful English-language destination for book series order queries.

Track:
- Organic traffic growth
- Number of ranking series pages
- Affiliate click-through rate
- Revenue per 1,000 sessions
- Returning visitor share

---

## Strategic Principles

1. **Search-led, not ad-driven.** Build for utility first; monetization is subordinate to user value.
2. **The series library is the moat.** It is evergreen, scalable, and defensible. Prioritise it.
3. **The bestseller feature is the brand.** It creates freshness and repeat habit.
4. **Amazon Associates is the revenue engine.** Keep it clean and compliant.
5. **Trust is a product feature.** Disclosure, methodology, and editorial standards are not afterthoughts.
